Muscle Fatigue Tracking Device

Arduino-based wearable for dancers and athletes

Overview

I designed and built a wearable device using Arduino Nano BLE Sense and an MPU-6050 motion sensor to measure muscle fatigue in real-time. This project combines my passion for dance and biomedical engineering by providing dancers with immediate feedback on body strain during practice.

How It Works

The sensor tracks motion and acceleration, while custom algorithms process the data to detect fatigue patterns. Data is streamed to a computer for visualization, enabling side-by-side comparisons of teacher and student movements.

Applications

  • Improving dance training by identifying strain and inefficiency
  • Supporting athletes with muscle fatigue monitoring
  • Potential applications in physical therapy and yoga training

Next Steps

I am refining the prototype, collecting feedback from dancers and yoga instructors, and exploring machine learning integration for more accurate fatigue detection.

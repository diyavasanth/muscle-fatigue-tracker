Muscle Fatigue Tracking Device

Arduino-based wearable providing real-time feedback for dancers

Overview

This project combines my passion for dance and biomedical engineering by creating a wearable device that monitors muscle fatigue in real-time. The goal is to help dancers optimize training, prevent injury, and understand their body's limits.

How It Works

The device uses an Arduino Nano BLE Sense with an MPU-6050 motion sensor to track arm movement and acceleration. Data is streamed to a computer for real-time visualization and analysis.

Real-Time Audio Feedback

The device provides immediate audio cues when a dancer’s arm moves outside the target range. This helps users correct posture and improve motion efficiency.

Future: integrate more advanced feedback algorithms using machine learning.

Motion Analysis & Comparison

Motion data is analyzed and displayed in graphs to compare student movements with the teacher’s reference. Key metrics include intensity, timing, and consistency.

Graphs will visualize real-time movement data for actionable insights.

Iterations & Learnings

Building the prototype involved multiple iterations. Some challenges included inaccurate sensor readings, calibration issues, and battery limitations.

Gotcha: Initially, the MPU-6050 produced noisy data when the arm moved quickly. This was solved by implementing a smoothing filter in software.
Gotcha: Battery life was too short during practice sessions. Switching to a higher-capacity LiPo solved this problem.

These iterations highlight problem-solving and engineering design skills.

User Feedback & Impact

“This device helped me notice subtle fatigue before it became a strain. I could adjust my technique instantly!” – Dance Instructor
“Visualizing my arm motion side-by-side with the teacher helped me improve faster than I expected.” – Student Dancer

Future Directions

  • Integrate machine learning to detect fatigue more accurately.
  • Expand device for full-body motion tracking.
